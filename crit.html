My work explores the relationship between Critical theory and counter-terrorism. With influences as diverse as Kafka and Miles Davis, new insights are crafted from both opaque and transparent meanings. Ever since I was a pre-adolescent I have been fascinated by the theoretical limits of relationships. What starts out as hope soon becomes corrupted into a dialectic of lust, leaving only a sense of what could have been and the dawn of a new order. As shimmering forms become reconfigured through frantic and repetitive practice, the viewer is left with a tribute to the limits of our existence.
